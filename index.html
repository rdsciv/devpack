§ 01 · The Loop

Pick a class.
Draw a mission.
Ship it.

A session runs 45 to 60 minutes. One mission per sitting. You win together, lose together, and your career builds across sessions — tech debt and all.

Turn 01
Pick a class.

Six decks. Frontend chains combos. Backend builds infrastructure. Data Sci queries, DevOps shields, ML trains. Design reframes. They actually play different.

Turn 02
Draw a mission.

Ship the MVP. Migrate the monolith. Launch before the embargo. The AI-DM generates missions on demand, or pull one from the deck and play analog.

Turn 03
Ship it.

Race the Ship Track before the Bug Track overflows. Shipped clean, shipped with debt, or an outright incident — each outcome rewrites your character.

§ 06 · Questions

Things people ask.

06.1
Do I need the app?
No. The physical game is self-contained. The AI-DM adds infinite missions and narrated Chaos, but you can run the whole thing from the decks with a human DM or no DM at all.
+
06.2
Is this an NFT? Any crypto?
No. Physical cards. Nothing on-chain. No wallets, no tokens, no royalties. If we ever add digital provenance it'll be for genuine authentication — never speculation.
+
06.3
Can I play solo?
Yes. Solo is first-class. The game was balanced around one-player play with the AI-DM acting as the Chaos engine, and scales cleanly to two or three.
+
06.4
When can I actually buy it?
Kickstarter is targeted for late 2026, with retail distribution in 2027. Waitlist members get a 72-hour early-access window before the Kickstarter goes public.
+
06.5
Is this another Magic clone?
No. No PvP. No combat. No mana. The design lineage is Gloomhaven and Pandemic — co-op, mission-based, narrative-forward. You're never playing against each other.
+
06.6
Who's behind it?
A small team of tabletop designers and working engineers. We are not a crypto company, a publisher's side project, or a venture-funded content mill. We're trying to make the card game we already wanted to play.
+
